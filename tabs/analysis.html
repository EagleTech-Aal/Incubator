Opioid abuse and opioid overdose deaths are a serious public health burden in the United States

  • 650,212 admissions to substance use treatment for opiates in 2018 (TEDS)
  • 46,802 opioid overdose deaths in 2018 (NCHS)

The opioid epidemic has occurred in parallel with the rise of social media. Our objective is to examine the relationships between data related to the opioid epidemic and the volume and polarity of Tweets published on Twitter. In order to accomplish this, we compared Tweets mentioning opioids with:

  • TEDS admissions for opioid use
  • Opioid overdose deaths reported by the National Center for Health Statistics

Our end goal is to provide a meaningful contribution to the discussion around the opioid epidemic and the role of social media in public health

Analysis

In order to compare tweets with TEDS admissions, tweets were assigned to opioid use categories analogous to the three codes representing opioid use in TEDS. Tweets mentioning heroin and methadone were assigned to individual categories, while tweets mentioning the remaining keywords were assigned to the “other opiates and synthetics” category. Summary datasets were created for both the Twitter data and TEDS data and then merged by opioid use category and year. The number of monthly active U.S. Twitter users (available beginning in 2010) was added to the dataset using supplementary data published by Twitter . We then created adjusted variables (tweets per 100,000 users and admissions per 100,000 total admissions) to better scale the variables and account for the exponential increase in Twitter activity over the last decade.

We assessed the relationship between tweets mentioning opioids and opioid overdose deaths using mortality data gathered from the National Vital Statistics System (NVSS) . Tweets per 100,000 users were compared to drug overdose death rates by opioid use category. For the purposes of this analysis, the opioid category analogous to “other opiates and synthetics” as reported by TEDS was “natural and semisynthetic opioids”, which includes such drugs as morphine, codeine, hydrocodone, and oxycodone.

# Variable Correlation Coefficient p-value
1 TEDS Heroin Admissions -0.76 0.03
2 TEDS Methadone Admissions 0.78 0.02
3 TEDS Other Opiates and Synthetics Admissions 0.83 0.01
4 Heroin Overdose Deaths -0.83 0.01
5 Methadone Overdose Deaths 0.95 <0.1
6 Natural and Semisynthetic Opioid Overdose Deaths -0.55 0.16

Correlation coefficients were used to measure the strength of the relationship between tweets and TEDS admissions. The methadone (r=0.78, p=0.02) and other opiate (r=0.83, p=0.01) categories showed significant positive relationships while we found a significant negative relationship for heroin (r=-0.76, p=0.03). The same method was used to measure the relationship between tweets and opioid overdose deaths. We found a strong positive relationship between tweets mentioning methadone and methadone overdose deaths (r=0.95, p<0.01) but observed weaker negative relationships for both heroin overdose deaths (-0.83, p=0.01) and natural and semisynthetic opioid overdose deaths (-0.55, p=0.16).
